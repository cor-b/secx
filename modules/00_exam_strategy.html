Exam Tips & Strategy

How to approach the SecurityX exam — distractors, key qualifier words, "best answer" logic, and what makes this cert different from earlier CompTIA exams.

overview

Every question is precisely worded and matches the study material — no trick questions. Read carefully, watch for distractors, and answer with the book answer, not your workplace experience. SecurityX leans heavily on scenario-based, hands-on application, not just identification.

the five strategy pillars

01 Read carefully — answer what's asked
02 Eliminate the distractor
03 Watch the qualifier words
04 Pick the most-right answer

01General Approach

  • No trick questions. Every question is precisely worded to match the study material.
  • Read each question multiple times to make sure you understand what's actually being asked.
  • Answer based on CompTIA SecurityX knowledge — not your personal workplace or school experience.
  • When in doubt, go with the book answer — that's what test writers use.

02Watch for Distractors

  • At least one of the four answer choices is usually a distractor / red herring.
  • Identifying and eliminating it dramatically improves your odds on the rest.
  • Treat elimination as a primary tool — narrow before you commit.

03Qualifier Words

Pay extra attention to words shown in bold, italics, or ALL CAPS — these signal critical qualifiers that change the answer.

Common qualifiers to watch

  • all, and, or, not
  • best
  • select two, select three
  • Other emphasis words with similar weight

04Choosing the Best Answer

  • Multiple answers may be technically correct — pick the most right (true most often, in most cases).
  • Don't overthink and pick a fringe-case answer.
  • Choose the answer correct in the highest number of situations.
  • If one option is specific and another is the generic category containing it → pick the specific one.

05Don't Fight the Exam

  • Identify the key concept being tested — don't argue with the question.
  • You don't need to memorize definitions word-for-word.
  • You just need to recognize correct answers, not regurgitate them.
  • Format: multiple-choice / multiple-select only — no fill-in-the-blank, no essays.

06What Makes SecurityX Different

Scenario weight
17 of 23 objectives (74%) are scenario-based
  • Heavy emphasis on the hands-on implementation of tools and concepts.
  • More operational / practical than A+, Network+, or Security+.
  • Expect to apply concepts with tools — not just identify use cases for them.
exam tips

★Key Takeaways

  • Read each question multiple times — the answer is in the wording.
  • Always look for and eliminate the distractor first.
  • Words like best, not, all, select two change the answer entirely — never skim them.
  • When two answers seem right, pick the one that's true most often, not the fringe case.
  • Trust the book answer over what your workplace does in practice.
  • You're being tested on recognition, not memorization or recitation.
quick decision rules
  • Two answers both look right → pick the most common / most-often-true one
  • One specific, one generic category → pick the specific one
  • Workplace says X, book says Y → pick Y
  • Question has bold / italic / ALL CAPS word → re-read it; that word is a qualifier
  • "Select two" or "select three" → partial credit doesn't apply — get the exact count right
