Operational Attack Surface

The vulnerabilities of day-to-day operations and people: risky user behavior, devices nobody approved, and accounts nobody is watching.

overview

The operational attack surface covers vulnerabilities from day-to-day operations and human factors. Three concepts: user factors (risks from users, weak passwords, phishing, privilege misuse), unsanctioned assets (unauthorized/unmanaged devices), and unsanctioned accounts (unauthorized or orphaned accounts).

concept 01

User Factors

Human risks: weak passwords, phishing susceptibility, privilege misuse. The weakest link. Fix: MFA, training, RBAC, activity monitoring.

concept 02

Unsanctioned Assets

Unauthorized/unmanaged devices lacking patches/AV. Discover with Nmap/Nessus/SolarWinds; enforce NAC.

concept 03

Unsanctioned Accounts

Orphaned/unauthorized accounts (ex-employees, temp projects). Audit and deactivate (Azure AD, Okta, SailPoint).

user factors

User factors are risks introduced by people: weak passwords, phishing susceptibility, and privilege misuse. Users are often the weakest link because behavior is unpredictable, a single phishing victim can lead to a breach, and privileged users (admins, executives) can misuse access (e.g. accidentally weakening firewall settings). Mitigate with MFA, strong password policies, security-awareness training (at onboarding and at least annually), phishing simulators, user-activity monitoring (login locations, unusual times), and role-based access control.

unsanctioned assets

Unsanctioned assets are unauthorized or unmanaged devices on the network (forgotten laptops, personal devices) that lack standardized security, updates, patches, or antivirus, becoming easy entry points. Example: an employee connecting an infected personal laptop to corporate Wi-Fi bypasses defenses. Discover them with network discovery/inventory tools (Nmap, Nessus, SolarWinds), enforce network access control (NAC) requiring devices to meet security standards, and keep asset inventories updated.

unsanctioned accounts

Unsanctioned accounts are unauthorized, orphaned, or unmanaged user accounts (left by departed employees, temporary projects, or created without approval). They retain access without monitoring, an active ex-employee account is a prime target, and they rarely get stronger security, providing a backdoor. Mitigate with periodic account audits, deactivating inactive accounts (Azure AD, Okta), identity governance (SailPoint, Oracle Identity Governance), and standardized onboarding/offboarding.

EXAM TIPS

what to know for the exam

  • Three operational concepts: user factors, unsanctioned assets, unsanctioned accounts.
  • User factors (humans) are often the weakest link; fix with MFA, training, RBAC, and phishing simulators.
  • Unsanctioned assets = unmanaged devices; discover with Nmap/Nessus/SolarWinds, enforce NAC.
  • Unsanctioned accounts = orphaned/unauthorized accounts; fix with audits, deactivation, and identity governance.
  • Standardized onboarding/offboarding reduces both unsanctioned assets and accounts.
decision rules
  • Scenario blames a breach on a phished employee → user factors
  • Scenario finds an unmanaged personal laptop on the network → unsanctioned asset
  • Scenario finds an active ex-employee account → unsanctioned account
  • Scenario runs Nmap to inventory connected devices → asset enumeration/discovery
