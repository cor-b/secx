Organizational Change Attack Surface

Transitions create gaps. New and departing staff, two companies becoming one, one company absorbing another, and a business splitting apart, each opens vulnerabilities if not carefully managed.

overview

The organizational change attack surface is vulnerabilities introduced during transition. Four concepts: staffing changes (onboarding/offboarding gaps), mergers (integrating two networks), acquisitions (taking control of another org), and divestitures (selling/separating a business unit).

change 01

Staffing Changes

Onboarding/offboarding gaps: orphaned accounts, excessive permissions. Fix: automated IAM (Okta, Azure AD).

change 02

Mergers

Integrating two networks; misaligned security protocols create gaps. Fix: security audit, segmentation, SIEM monitoring.

change 03

Acquisitions

One org takes over another; acquired infra may have hidden vulns/differing standards. Fix: assess (Rapid7, Nessus), review access.

change 04

Divestitures

Selling/separating a unit; exposed data or improperly decommissioned assets. Fix: DLP, decommission audits.

staffing changes and mergers

Staffing changes (onboarding/offboarding) create access-control gaps, orphaned accounts retaining access after departure, or new employees granted excessive permissions. Mitigate with automated IAM (Okta, Azure AD) to streamline provisioning and quickly deactivate accounts. Mergers integrate two companies' networks/systems; rushing to consolidate without aligning security protocols leaves resources vulnerable (employees from one company gaining access to the other's confidential data). Mitigate with a comprehensive security audit, network segmentation (Cisco ISE, firewalls), and SIEM monitoring for abnormal activity (unusual logins, excessive transfers).

acquisitions and divestitures

Acquisitions (one org taking control of another) carry the risk that the acquired infrastructure has vulnerabilities or differing security standards, e.g. outdated services now exposed to the combined network. Mitigate with a security assessment of acquired systems (Rapid7, Nessus) before integration and careful access-control review. Divestitures (selling or separating a business unit) can expose sensitive data or leave assets insufficiently protected; e.g. failing to decommission shared servers leaves the separated entity with access. Mitigate with data loss prevention tools (Symantec DLP, McAfee Total Protection) and a security audit confirming all shared resources are reconfigured or decommissioned.

EXAM TIPS

what to know for the exam

  • Four change types: staffing changes, mergers, acquisitions, divestitures.
  • Staffing changes → orphaned accounts/excessive permissions; fix with automated IAM and offboarding.
  • Mergers integrate two networks (align protocols, segment, monitor); acquisitions inherit the other org's vulnerabilities (assess first).
  • Divestitures risk exposed data and un-decommissioned assets; fix with DLP and decommission audits.
  • Tools by phase: IAM (staffing), SIEM/segmentation (mergers), Rapid7/Nessus (acquisitions), DLP (divestitures).
decision rules
  • Scenario leaves an ex-employee's account active → staffing change
  • Scenario integrates two companies' networks with misaligned security → merger
  • Scenario inherits an acquired company's outdated, exposed services → acquisition
  • Scenario fails to decommission shared servers when splitting off a unit → divestiture
