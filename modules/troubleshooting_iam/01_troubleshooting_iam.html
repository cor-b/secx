Troubleshooting IAM

Right people, right access, right resources, right time. Managing identities and controlling who and what can reach enterprise resources, on-prem and in the cloud.

overview

Domain 3 (Security Engineering), Objective 3.1: troubleshoot common issues with identity and access management components in an enterprise environment. IAM ensures the right people have the right access to the right resources at the right time, through management frameworks, subject and user identity controls, secrets management, authentication/authorization protocols, cloud IAM policies, WiFi auth, access control, conditional access, and logging/monitoring.

area 01

Identity & Subjects

Management frameworks (proofing, PIM), subject access control (users/processes/devices/services), user identity control (credentials, biometrics, MFA), secrets management.

area 02

Auth Protocols

Authentication and authorization (SAML, OpenID, OAuth, federation), cloud IAM access and trust policies, WiFi authentication (802.1X, EAP, SAE).

area 03

Control & Oversight

Access control (SSO, Kerberos, PAM), conditional access (binding, time, location), logging and monitoring.
