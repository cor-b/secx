Cyber Kill Chain

Lockheed Martin's seven sequential phases of an attack. Break any single link and the whole attack fails, forcing the adversary back to step one.

overview

The Cyber Kill Chain, developed by Lockheed Martin, breaks an attack into seven sequential steps; each must occur in order for the attack to succeed. Breaking any link disrupts the whole attack, forcing the attacker to start over. The steps: reconnaissance, weaponization, delivery, exploitation, installation, command and control, actions on objectives.

the seven steps

  1. reconnaissance Gather information about the target (OSINT, scanning) to find vulnerabilities and entry points. Passive or active.
  2. weaponization Create a malicious payload/exploit tailored to the discovered weakness, often tested in a controlled environment.
  3. delivery Transmit the payload to the target (phishing email, compromised website, infected USB). First direct interaction with the target.
  4. exploitation The payload executes, exploiting a vulnerability to gain access (e.g. victim clicks a malicious link/attachment).
  5. installation Malware establishes itself, creating a backdoor or persistence mechanism for continued access.
  6. command and control (C2) A communication channel connects the attacker to the compromised system for remote control; often encrypted/concealed.
  7. actions on objectives The attacker achieves their goal: data theft, ransomware, sabotage, espionage.

breaking the chain and the six Ds

Defenders can disrupt an attack at any step. Detecting suspicious emails at the delivery stage lets a team block the sender, add filters, and alert users, stopping exploitation and later phases. Even at the C2 stage, monitoring traffic for connections to unfamiliar servers lets defenders cut the channel. The kill chain aligns with the six Ds of defense: detect, deny, disrupt, degrade, deceive, destroy (e.g. deceive with a honeypot, degrade by throttling network speed to slow exfiltration).

EXAM TIPS

what to know for the exam

  • Seven steps in order: reconnaissance, weaponization, delivery, exploitation, installation, command and control, actions on objectives.
  • Developed by Lockheed Martin; steps are sequential, breaking one link stops the attack.
  • Delivery is the first step where the attacker directly interacts with the target.
  • The six Ds of defense: detect, deny, disrupt, degrade, deceive, destroy.
  • Detecting and blocking phishing at delivery prevents exploitation and everything after.
decision rules
  • Scenario gathers target info before attacking → reconnaissance
  • Scenario emails a malicious attachment → delivery
  • Scenario establishes a remote control channel → command and control
  • Scenario deploys a honeypot to mislead the attacker → deceive (six Ds)
