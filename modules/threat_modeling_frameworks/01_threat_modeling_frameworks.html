Threat Modeling Frameworks

Structured ways to understand and defend against attacks: the kill chain's sequential phases, MITRE's attack catalogs, the diamond's intrusion components, STRIDE's threat categories, and OWASP's web-app risks.

overview

Domain 1 (Governance, Risk, and Compliance), Objective 1.4: perform threat modeling activities. Frameworks give different perspectives, from categorizing attack patterns to mapping intrusion stages, helping teams prioritize defenses. The section covers the Cyber Kill Chain, CAPEC, MITRE ATT&CK, the Diamond Model, STRIDE, and OWASP.

group 01

Lifecycle & Patterns

Cyber Kill Chain (7 sequential phases) and CAPEC (catalog of common attack patterns).

group 02

Adversary Mapping

MITRE ATT&CK (TTP matrices + Navigator) and the Diamond Model (adversary, infrastructure, capability, victim).

group 03

Design & Web

STRIDE (six threat categories for design) and OWASP (Top 10, ASVS, ZAP for web apps).
