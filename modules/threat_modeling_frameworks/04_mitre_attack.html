MITRE ATT&CK

A comprehensive catalog of real-world adversary tactics, techniques, and procedures, organized into matrices, with a Navigator tool to map a specific threat actor against your defenses.

overview

The MITRE ATT&CK (Adversarial Tactics, Techniques, and Common Knowledge) framework documents the TTPs adversaries use, organized into matrices with entries detailing required permissions, affected platforms, and mitigations. Matrices: Enterprise, Mobile, PRE-ATT&CK, and ICS. The ATT&CK Navigator visually maps techniques to specific threat actors.

matrix 01

Enterprise / Mobile

Enterprise: desktops, servers, cloud (most used). Mobile: attacks on mobile devices/apps/OSes.

matrix 02

PRE / ICS

PRE-ATT&CK: pre-attack behavior (recon, planning). ICS: industrial control systems (energy, manufacturing).

tool

ATT&CK Navigator

Visually map a threat actor's techniques onto a matrix to find gaps in your defenses.

the matrices

ATT&CK organizes TTPs into matrices for different environments:

  • Enterprise ATT&CK: the most widely used, covering desktops, servers, and cloud.
  • ATT&CK for Mobile: tactics/techniques against mobile devices, apps, and OSes.
  • PRE-ATT&CK: adversary behavior before an attack (reconnaissance, planning).
  • ATT&CK for ICS: techniques targeting industrial environments (energy, manufacturing).

the ATT&CK Navigator

The ATT&CK Navigator is a visual tool to map and analyze how specific techniques align with real-world threat actors. Example workflow: research a threat actor like APT29 (attributed to Russia's SVR), overlay its known techniques onto the Enterprise matrix, color-coded (e.g. techniques used directly vs in attributed campaigns), then review your own infrastructure to identify high-probability targets and gaps. This lets a team prioritize hardening and detection for the techniques that specific adversary actually uses, e.g. preparing for credential dumping by checking ATT&CK for the technique, mapping it against current defenses, and adding alerts or restricting admin permissions where coverage is missing.

EXAM TIPS

what to know for the exam

  • ATT&CK documents adversary TTPs (tactics, techniques, procedures) in matrices.
  • Four matrices: Enterprise (desktops/servers/cloud), Mobile, PRE-ATT&CK (pre-attack recon/planning), ICS.
  • The ATT&CK Navigator visually maps a specific threat actor's techniques onto a matrix to find defensive gaps.
  • Use case: map a known APT (e.g. APT29) against your defenses and prioritize hardening.
  • CAPEC = attack patterns for dev; ATT&CK = real-world adversary TTPs for defense mapping.
decision rules
  • Scenario catalogs real-world adversary TTPs in matrices → MITRE ATT&CK
  • Scenario visually maps APT29 techniques to find gaps → ATT&CK Navigator
  • Scenario needs pre-attack reconnaissance behavior → PRE-ATT&CK matrix
  • Scenario covers industrial control systems → ATT&CK for ICS
