PKI Architecture

The framework of digital trust: who issues certificates, what's in them, how they're checked for validity, and how they get deployed everywhere.

overview

Domain 2, Objective 2.4: apply security concepts to the design of access, authentication, and authorization systems. Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) is the backbone of secure online communication, a framework for managing digital certificates. Trusted certificate authorities issue and manage them, validation confirms they remain trustworthy, and deployment pushes them across servers and devices.

topic 01

Certificate Management

The certificate lifecycle: certificate types (TLS, client, code-signing, wildcard, SAN) and certificate extensions.

topic 02

CA Functions

Certificate authorities (root, intermediate, chain of trust) and registration authorities that verify requesters via a CSR.

topic 03

Certificate Validation

OCSP stapling, certificate revocation lists (CRL), and certificate pinning, layered for trust.

topic 04

Certificate Deployment

Templates for consistency, deployment approach (manual vs automated), and integration into existing systems.
