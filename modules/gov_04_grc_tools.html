GRC Tools

Integrated platforms that automate governance, risk, and compliance activities: mapping controls to regulations, tracking obligations, and monitoring continuously.

overview

GRC tools exist to reduce the manual burden of compliance. They integrate governance, risk management, and regulatory compliance into a single platform with five core capabilities: documentation, mapping, compliance tracking, automation, and continuous monitoring.

Documentation

Stores and organizes policies, procedures, and rules in one central location, making them easy to retrieve during an audit.

Example tool
RSA Archer documentation Use case: a telecom company stores data protection policies. Auditors can pull them instantly rather than hunting through shared drives.

Mapping

Links a company's internal processes and controls to external regulations and standards. Also helps identify gaps: processes that exist internally but don't satisfy a regulatory requirement.

Example tool
SAP GRC mapping Use case: mapping company activities to GDPR requirements, showing which controls satisfy which articles, and flagging gaps.

GDPR

General Data Protection Regulation. EU law with strict guidelines on collecting, processing, and protecting personal data. Gives individuals greater control over personal information. Significant penalties for noncompliance.

Compliance Tracking

Monitors whether the company is following regulations and internal policies. Sends alerts when audits or reviews are due, preventing obligations from slipping through the cracks.

Example tool
ServiceNow GRC compliance tracking Use case: a financial institution tracks its SOX compliance obligations with automated reminders before audit deadlines.

SOX

Sarbanes-Oxley Act. U.S. federal law imposing strict financial reporting and auditing requirements on public companies. Protects investors from fraud and ensures accurate corporate disclosures.

Automation

Completes repetitive compliance tasks (control testing, evidence collection, report generation) without manual effort. Frees security teams to focus on analysis rather than paperwork.

Example tool
SAP GRC automation Use case: an energy company automates quarterly control testing and generates compliance reports automatically.

Controls = policies, procedures, and mechanisms put in place to manage risk, ensure compliance, and protect assets. GRC automation handles the ongoing testing and documentation of these controls.

Continuous Monitoring

Real-time tracking of risks and compliance issues with immediate alerts when something falls out of compliance. Shifts from point-in-time audits to always-on awareness.

Example tool
Qualys continuous monitoring Use case: a retail company monitors payment systems for security threats in real time, helping maintain PCI-DSS compliance continuously rather than only during annual assessments.

exam tips

Key Takeaways

  • Know all four tools and their primary feature: RSA Archer, SAP GRC, ServiceNow GRC, Qualys.
  • GDPR = EU, personal data. SOX = US, public company financials. PCI-DSS = global, contractual, payment card data.
  • Most GRC tools do all five features. The lesson pairs each tool with one feature for memorization, not exclusivity.
quick decision rules
  • Audit prep, finding policies fast → Documentation (RSA Archer)
  • Aligning internal processes to a regulation → Mapping (SAP GRC)
  • Alert when audit deadline approaches → Compliance tracking (ServiceNow GRC)
  • Eliminate manual control testing → Automation (SAP GRC)
  • "Real-time" or "immediate alert" → Continuous monitoring (Qualys)
  • EU personal data law → GDPR
  • US public company financial auditing → SOX
