Management Involvement

Effective governance requires leadership's active participation. The RACI matrix is the primary tool for clarifying exactly who does what.

overview

Without management engagement, key decisions get delayed, resources become insufficient, and projects lose alignment with business goals. The RACI matrix ensures every task has a clear owner, executor, advisor, and audience, eliminating ambiguity across governance structures.

the four RACI roles

R Responsible Does the actual work. Executes the task. Can be multiple people.
A Accountable Owns the outcome. Final authority. Only ONE person per task.
C Consulted Provides expert input before decisions. Advisory role; does not execute.
I Informed Kept up to date on progress. Receives updates only, no input given.

The RACI Matrix

A table mapping tasks to roles; every intersection is labeled R, A, C, or I. Rows = tasks or deliverables. Columns = roles or individuals.

Responsible (R)

  • The person or team doing the actual work
  • Executes plans, meets deadlines, drives the task forward day-to-day
  • Can be multiple people on a single task

Accountable (A)

  • Has final authority over the task's outcome. The buck stops here.
  • Ensures goals are met, but may not personally do the work
  • Only ONE person per task. This is the most important rule.
  • Multiple accountable people = no clear ownership = confusion

Consulted (C)

  • Stakeholders or experts whose input is sought before decisions
  • Advisory role: provides guidance and expertise
  • Does not execute; influences direction
  • Often subject matter experts outside the core team

Informed (I)

  • Kept up to date on progress and outcomes
  • Not involved in execution or decision-making
  • Receives updates only, provides no input
  • Ensures transparency and visibility across the organization

Why Management Commitment Matters

Without it

  • Key decisions get delayed; no one with authority is engaged
  • Resources may be insufficient; no executive sponsorship to unlock budget
  • Alignment with business goals becomes unclear
  • Projects risk delay, misalignment, and failure

With it

  • Roles are properly assigned and understood
  • Accountability is enforced at the right level
  • Projects receive the support and oversight they need
  • Governance structures are actually implemented, not just documented
exam tips

Key Takeaways

  • Only ONE accountable person per task. This is the most common trap question.
  • R = doer, A = owner of the outcome, C = advisor, I = update recipient.
  • Multiple people can share the R role; only A is strictly one person.
  • The RACI matrix is one tool. Others exist, but RACI is the CompTIA example.
quick decision rules
  • "Approves" or "has final say" → Accountable (A)
  • "Performs" or "executes" → Responsible (R)
  • "Provides expert input or feedback" → Consulted (C)
  • "Is notified" or "kept aware" → Informed (I)
  • Who can there only be one of? → Accountable (A)
