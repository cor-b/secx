Change & Configuration Management

Structured processes for managing system modifications, ensuring changes are carried out efficiently, approved, and tracked accurately.

overview

Change and configuration management ensures system modifications are carried out efficiently, approved, and tracked accurately. Built on three critical elements.

the three critical elements

01 Inventory
02 Asset Management Lifecycle
03 Configuration Management Database

Inventory

Purpose

Provides a complete and accurate record of all hardware, software, and other assets in the organization, including:

  • What assets are in use
  • Where they're located
  • Their current configuration

Why it matters

  • Lets IT teams assess the impact of proposed changes
  • Prevents disruptions during updates and maintenance
  • Ensures no critical systems are overlooked during upgrades
  • Helps with troubleshooting: quickly isolate issues to specific assets
Example tool
Microsoft SCCM System Center Configuration Manager
  • Tracks hardware specs, configurations, and locations
  • Monitors installed software, versions, and licensing
  • Pushes updates, patches, and new software across devices

Asset Management Lifecycle

The five phases

  1. Planning & Procurement Acquire assets that align with organizational needs.
  2. Deployment Integrate assets into the environment; ensure they work with existing systems.
  3. Utilization & Maintenance Apply regular updates and patches to keep assets functional and secure.
  4. Asset Tracking Document changes and manage assets throughout their use.
  5. Decommissioning & Disposal Retire outdated assets in a controlled, secure manner.
Example tool
ServiceNow IT Asset Management

Configuration Management Database

What it is

A centralized database that tracks all Configuration Items (CIs) in the organization.

Configuration Items (CIs)

Any asset, component, or service that must be managed to deliver IT services. Examples include:

  • Servers
  • Desktops
  • Mobile phones
  • Software
  • Databases
  • Facility infrastructure

What the CMDB tracks

  • How each CI is set up, connected, or used at a specific point in time
  • Relationships between CIs: how one CI connects to or depends on others
  • How each CI connects to specific services

Why relationships matter

  • Lets IT teams predict the impact of changes; if you change CI A, what happens to CI B?
  • Helps manage interdependencies and risk
  • Critical for network stability

When the CMDB must be updated

Anytime a CI's configuration changes:

  • Software update
  • Hardware replacement
  • Network modification
Example tool
ServiceNow CMDB

exam tips

Key Takeaways

  • Inventory = what assets you have (and where).
  • Asset Management Lifecycle = managing assets from cradle to grave (5 phases: plan → deploy → use → track → dispose).
  • CMDB = central database of CIs and their relationships to each other and to services.
  • Configuration Item (CI) = any managed asset, component, or service.
  • The CMDB's biggest value: showing interdependencies so you can predict the impact of a change.
quick decision rules
  • Impact of changes between systems → CMDB
  • Tracking what you own → Inventory
  • Asset's lifespan from procurement to disposal → Asset Management Lifecycle
