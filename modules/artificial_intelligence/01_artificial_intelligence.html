Artificial Intelligence

The security challenges of adopting AI: what it can build for you, how to govern it responsibly, and the new attack surface it opens up, from poisoned models to deepfakes.

overview

Domains 1 and 3, Objectives 1.5 (summarize the information security challenges of AI adoption) and 3.6 (use automation to secure the enterprise). AI brings power and productivity but also ethical concerns, legal/privacy risks, new vulnerabilities, and malicious misuse. The section covers generative AI, ethical/governance considerations, legal and privacy risks, threats to the model, AI-enabled attacks, AI manipulation attacks, AI usage risks, and AI bots.

area 01

Use & Govern

Generative AI (content, code assist, docs), ethical/governance considerations, and legal/privacy risks.

area 02

Attacks

Threats to the model (prompt injection, poisoning), AI-enabled attacks (automated exploits), and manipulation (deepfakes).

area 03

Risk & Control

AI usage risks (overreliance, disclosure, excessive agency) and AI bots (access, guardrails, DLP, disclosure).
