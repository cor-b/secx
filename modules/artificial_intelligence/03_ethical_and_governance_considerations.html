Ethical and Governance Considerations

Keeping AI fair, transparent, and accountable: the broad frameworks that govern responsible AI, and the internal company policies that put them into practice.

overview

Ethical and governance considerations establish frameworks and guidelines so AI is developed and used responsibly, aligned with societal and organizational values. Two concepts: the ethical governance of AI (structures ensuring fairness, transparency, accountability, e.g. the EU AI Act) and organizational policies on AI use (internal company rules aligning AI with legal and ethical standards).

concept 01

Ethical Governance

Broad structures for fair, transparent, accountable AI. EU AI Act (risk tiers, bias testing) good; COMPAS recidivism tool bad.

concept 02

Organizational Policies

Internal company rules for AI use. Microsoft AI ethics review good; Amazon's gender-biased hiring tool bad. Need transparency + training.

ethical governance of AI

Ethical governance establishes structures ensuring AI is used responsibly, prioritizing fairness, transparency, and accountability. The EU AI Act is a leading example: it categorizes AI systems into risk levels and, for high-risk applications (like facial recognition), requires bias testing, transparent documentation of data/algorithms/decisions for audits, and human oversight. A failure example is COMPAS (Correctional Offender Management Profiling for Alternative Sanctions), a US recidivism-risk algorithm deployed without bias testing or transparency that overestimated recidivism risk for minority defendants, causing racial disparities; governance mandating bias audits could have caught it. Effective governance prioritizes transparency (understanding the decision process) and accountability (clear responsibility and protocols to investigate and correct errors).

organizational policies on AI use

Organizational policies are internal rules controlling how AI is used within a company, aligning it with ethical standards and legal obligations. Microsoft, for example, has AI ethics principles requiring expert review of high-impact systems. A cautionary case is Amazon's discontinued AI hiring tool, which exhibited gender bias because it was trained on historical data favoring male candidates, showing why policies must mandate fairness checks. Good policies need:

  • Transparency: clear ethical boundaries and acceptable practices for data and AI use.
  • Training and education: teach employees AI ethics (data privacy, algorithmic bias, transparency).
  • Clear guidelines and continuous training: reinforce accountability at all levels.
EXAM TIPS

what to know for the exam

  • Ethical governance = broad structures for fair, transparent, accountable AI; organizational policies = internal company rules.
  • EU AI Act categorizes AI by risk level and mandates bias testing/transparency for high-risk uses.
  • COMPAS (recidivism) is the governance-failure example (racial bias, no transparency).
  • Amazon's biased hiring tool shows why internal policies must require fairness checks.
  • Transparency + accountability are the pillars of effective AI governance.
decision rules
  • Scenario sets society-wide rules with risk tiers → ethical governance (EU AI Act)
  • Scenario writes internal company AI rules → organizational policy
  • Scenario's recidivism model shows racial bias unchecked → governance failure (COMPAS)
  • Scenario's hiring AI favors one gender from biased data → need for fairness-check policy
