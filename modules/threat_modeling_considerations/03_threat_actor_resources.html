Threat Actor Resources

What an attacker has to work with. Two resources separate opportunistic dabblers from sophisticated campaigns: the time to be patient, and the money to buy capability.

overview

Threat actor resources are the assets available to plan, execute, and sustain an attack. Two key resources: time (well-funded actors run prolonged, patient campaigns over months or years) and money (buys advanced tools, skilled personnel, zero-day exploits, and infrastructure).

resource 01

Time

Enables prolonged, patient campaigns (months/years). SolarWinds attackers spent a year. Defeats periodic scans, demands behavioral/continuous monitoring.

resource 02

Money

Buys advanced tools, skilled personnel, zero-days, infrastructure, insiders. Lazarus's $81M Bangladesh Bank heist. Raises attack sophistication.

time

Time lets well-funded attackers (like nation-states) conduct prolonged campaigns over months or years, slowly collecting data, studying vulnerabilities, and developing tailored strategies, acting strategically rather than opportunistically. The SolarWinds breach exemplifies this: attackers spent over a year developing and deploying the backdoor, maintaining covert, persistent access. This patience is dangerous because standard periodic scans may miss well-hidden intrusions, so defenders need behavioral analysis, anomaly detection, and continuous monitoring rather than relying on single-instance alerts.

money

Money enables attackers to buy advanced tools, skilled personnel, and zero-day exploits (unknown to vendors, harder to defend), rent command-and-control infrastructure, and recruit insiders. The North Korean group Lazarus reportedly used extensive funding for large-scale operations like the 2016 Bangladesh Bank heist ($81 million stolen via SWIFT). Financial backing raises an attack's sophistication and persistence. Knowing this, organizations should secure high-value assets, employ multi-layered defenses, and invest in proactive threat hunting and incident response that can withstand well-funded attacks.

EXAM TIPS

what to know for the exam

  • Two resources: time and money. Both increase an attack's sophistication and persistence.
  • Time = patient, prolonged campaigns (SolarWinds, ~1 year); defeats periodic scans → use continuous/behavioral monitoring.
  • Money = advanced tools, skilled personnel, zero-days, infrastructure, insiders (Lazarus / Bangladesh Bank $81M).
  • Well-funded, patient actors are typically nation-states or sophisticated groups.
decision rules
  • Scenario maintains covert access for a year before acting → time as a resource
  • Scenario buys a zero-day or hires specialists → money as a resource
  • Scenario evades periodic scans via slow, hidden activity → time (use continuous monitoring)
  • Scenario funds global SWIFT fraud → money (Lazarus)
