Threat Modeling Considerations

Knowing your adversary: who would target you and why, what they have to work with, how skilled they are, and the patterns and methods they use, from first access to covering their tracks.

overview

Domains 1 and 3, Objectives 1.4 (perform threat modeling activities) and 3.2 (analyze requirements to enhance endpoint/server security). Threat modeling identifies and evaluates potential threats by understanding who might attack, why, and how. The section covers threat actor motivation, resources, and capabilities, then attack patterns, threat actor methods, and the attack lifecycle: initial access/escalation and post-exploitation/evasion.

area 01

The Adversary

Threat actor motivation (why), resources (time, money), and capabilities (skills, tools, supply-chain access).

area 02

The Approach

Attack patterns (injection, auth, on-path) and threat actor methods (abuse cases, anti-patterns, attack trees).

area 03

The Lifecycle

Initial access and escalation (injection, credential dumping, privilege escalation) and post-exploitation/evasion (lateral movement, execution, evasion).
