Every acronym and key term across all sections, in one place. Use Ctrl+F to find anything fast.

how to use

The acronym index covers every abbreviation that appears in the lessons, with its expansion and a one-line meaning. The term glossary below it collects the high-value and easily-confused concepts. This page is built for lookup: jump by letter, or just search the page (Ctrl+F / Cmd+F).

acronym expansion meaning
A
ABACAttribute-Based Access ControlGrants access dynamically from user, resource, and environment attributes (time, location, device).
ACLAccess Control ListRule-based list defining which traffic or users are permitted or denied.
AES-256Advanced Encryption Standard (256-bit)Symmetric encryption standard for data at rest and in VPN tunnels.
AICPAAmerican Institute of Certified Public AccountantsBody that developed the SOC 2 auditing framework.
ALEAnnualized Loss ExpectancyExpected yearly loss from a risk: SLE × ARO.
APOAlign, Plan, OrganizeCOBIT management domain for strategic planning and alignment.
APIApplication Programming InterfaceInterface for integrating external services with internal systems.
AROAnnualized Rate of OccurrenceHow many times per year a threat is expected to occur.
ASVApproved Scanning VendorPCI DSS vendor authorized to run quarterly external vulnerability scans.
AVAsset ValueMonetary value of an asset in quantitative risk analysis.
B
BAIBuild, Acquire, ImplementCOBIT management domain for delivering new capabilities and changes.
BC/DRBusiness Continuity / Disaster RecoveryPlanning to keep operations running and to restore them after disruption.
BIABusiness Impact AnalysisProcess evaluating the effect of disruptions on mission-essential functions.
BIOSBasic Input/Output SystemLegacy system firmware that UEFI replaces; less secure, no Secure Boot.
BPABusiness Partner AgreementContract governing strategic partnerships (joint ventures, revenue sharing).
C
CAIQConsensus Assessment Initiative QuestionnaireCSA template for standardized cloud vendor security questionnaires.
CCCommon CriteriaInternational standard for evaluating hardware/software security (EAL 1–7).
CCMCloud Controls MatrixCSA control catalog used in STAR assessments, mapped to other frameworks.
CCPACalifornia Consumer Privacy ActCalifornia privacy law granting residents rights over personal data.
CERCrossover Error RateBiometric tuning point where FAR equals FRR; lower is better.
CIConfiguration ItemAny asset or component tracked in a CMDB.
CI/CDContinuous Integration / Continuous DeploymentAutomated pipeline for integrating, testing, and deploying code.
CISCenter for Internet SecurityPublisher of prescriptive hardening benchmarks and security controls.
CISOChief Information Security OfficerExecutive who sets security strategy, budget, and risk appetite.
CMDBConfiguration Management DatabaseCentral database tracking configuration items and their relationships.
CMMCCybersecurity Maturity Model CertificationTiered maturity model required of defense contractors handling CUI.
CMMICapability Maturity Model IntegrationFive-level process maturity model (ad hoc to optimized).
COBITControl Objectives for Information and Related TechnologiesIT governance framework: 5 domains, 40 objectives, aligns IT to business.
COPPAChildren's Online Privacy Protection ActUS law protecting data of children under 13, enforced by the FTC.
COSOCommittee of Sponsoring OrganizationsEnterprise risk management and internal controls framework.
CSACloud Security AllianceBody providing cloud security guidance, the CCM, and STAR certification.
CUIControlled Unclassified InformationSensitive but unclassified data, legally protected in defense contracts.
CVECommon Vulnerabilities and ExposuresStandard identifier for a publicly known vulnerability.
CVSSCommon Vulnerability Scoring SystemStandardized 0–10 severity score for vulnerabilities.
D
DACDiscretionary Access ControlResource owner controls access; default in Windows and Linux.
DASTDynamic Application Security TestingTests a running app from the outside (ZAP, Burp, fuzzing).
DDoSDistributed Denial of ServiceFlooding a service with traffic from many sources to take it down.
DLPData Loss PreventionDetects and blocks unauthorized movement of sensitive data.
DMADigital Markets ActEU regulation targeting large platform "gatekeepers."
DMZDemilitarized ZoneBuffer zone (screened subnet) between trusted internal and untrusted external networks.
DPAData Processing AgreementGDPR-required contract between data controller and processor.
DPOData Protection OfficerIndependent role GDPR mandates for certain organizations.
DSSDeliver, Service, SupportCOBIT management domain for day-to-day IT operations.
E
EALEvaluation Assurance LevelCommon Criteria assurance rating from EAL1 to EAL7.
ECCElliptic Curve CryptographyPublic-key crypto; one of the key types an HSM can generate.
EDMEvaluate, Direct, MonitorCOBIT governance domain (distinct from its management domains).
EDREndpoint Detection and ResponseEndpoint security tooling, often with built-in file integrity monitoring.
EFExposure FactorPercentage of asset value lost if a threat is realized.
EHRElectronic Health RecordsDigital health records incentivized under HITECH.
EOLEnd of LifeVendor stops selling/developing; patches may still exist at a premium.
EOSLEnd of Service LifeAll support and patches end; critical security threshold.
EOSEnd of SupportVendor stops issuing patches and security updates.
ePHIElectronic Protected Health InformationDigital PHI regulated under HIPAA and HITECH.
F
FAIRFactor Analysis of Information RiskQuantitative framework expressing cyber risk in financial terms.
FARFalse Acceptance RateRate at which a biometric system accepts unauthorized users.
FERCFederal Energy Regulatory CommissionEnforces compliance (including NERC-CIP) in the energy sector.
FIMFile Integrity MonitoringDetects unauthorized changes to files (Tripwire, AIDE).
FIPSFederal Information Processing StandardUS gov standard; FIPS 140-2 governs cryptographic modules and SEDs.
FISMAFederal Information Security Modernization ActRequires federal agencies to run information security programs.
FRRFalse Rejection RateRate at which a biometric system rejects legitimate users.
FTCFederal Trade CommissionEnforces COPPA and consumer privacy regulations.
G
GDPRGeneral Data Protection RegulationEU privacy law with extraterritorial reach and steep penalties.
GLBAGramm-Leach-Bliley ActUS law requiring financial institutions to protect customer data.
GRCGovernance, Risk, and ComplianceIntegrated platform category for policy, compliance, and monitoring.
H
HIDSHost-based Intrusion Detection SystemIDS deployed on an individual endpoint.
HIPAAHealth Insurance Portability and Accountability ActUS law protecting patient health information (PHI).
HITECHHealth Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health ActStrengthens HIPAA and incentivizes EHR adoption.
HSMHardware Security ModuleDedicated device for generating and protecting cryptographic keys.
I
IaaSInfrastructure as a ServiceCloud model: provider manages hardware; customer manages OS and up.
IAMIdentity and Access ManagementUnified authentication and authorization across environments.
IASTInteractive Application Security TestingAgent inside a running app during functional testing (combines SAST+DAST).
IdPIdentity ProviderAuthenticates users on behalf of service providers (Google, Azure AD).

IEEEInstitute of Electrical and Electronics EngineersStandards body; IEEE 1667 covers secure storage authentication for SEDs.
IoTInternet of ThingsNetworked physical devices; subject of the IoT Cybersecurity Act.
IPSIntrusion Prevention SystemActive device that detects, alerts, and blocks threats inline.

ISAInterconnection Security AgreementTechnical/security contract for securely connecting two networks.
ISMSInformation Security Management SystemStandardized approach to managing security controls (ISO 27001).
ISOInternational Organization for StandardizationDevelops international standards including the 27000 series.
ISSOInformation Systems Security OfficerSystem-level security officer role in NIST 800-53 / federal contexts.
J
JSONJavaScript Object NotationKey-value data format; used by JWTs and many logs.
JWTJSON Web TokenCompact signed token carrying identity claims between IdP and SP.

KDCKey Distribution CenterKerberos component that issues TGTs and service tickets.

LGPDLei Geral de Proteção de DadosBrazil's data protection law, modeled on GDPR.
LUKSLinux Unified Key SetupLinux disk-encryption key management, used via Cryptsetup (AES).

MEAMonitor, Evaluate, AssessCOBIT management domain for performance and compliance monitoring.
MEFMission-Essential FunctionCore activity an organization must sustain; identified in a BIA.

NIST CSFNIST Cybersecurity FrameworkOutcomes-based framework: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover (+ Govern).
NIST RMFNIST Risk Management FrameworkCategorize, select, implement, assess, authorize, monitor.

O
OAuthOpen AuthorizationFramework for delegating API access without sharing credentials.
OCTAVEOperationally Critical Threat, Asset, and Vulnerability EvaluationHands-on, operations-focused IT risk assessment framework.

OPSECOperational SecurityControlling dissemination of sensitive operational information.
OSIOpen Systems InterconnectionSeven-layer model describing network communication.
OWASPOpen Worldwide Application Security ProjectCommunity behind ZAP, Dependency-Check, and the Top 10.
P
PaaSPlatform as a ServiceCloud model: provider manages hardware/OS; customer manages apps/data.
PAMPrivileged Access ManagementSystem for managing and monitoring elevated-account access.
PCI DSSPayment Card Industry Data Security StandardContractual standard for protecting cardholder data.
PDPPolicy Decision PointEvaluates an access request against policy and decides allow/deny (AWS IAM).
PEPPolicy Enforcement PointActs on the PDP's decision to grant or block access (Palo Alto NGFW).
PHIProtected Health InformationPatient health data regulated under HIPAA.
PIIPersonally Identifiable InformationData that can identify a specific individual.
PRIPriority (Syslog)Syslog code: (facility × 8) + severity.
Q
QAQuality AssurancePhase/environment validating a system against standards before release.
QoSQuality of ServicePrioritizing and managing traffic flow.
QSAQualified Security AssessorIndependent auditor for on-site PCI DSS Level 1 audits.
R
RACIResponsible, Accountable, Consulted, InformedMatrix clarifying roles and responsibilities for tasks.
RAIDRedundant Array of Independent DisksDisk redundancy; self-healing servers auto-rebuild a failed drive.
RASPRuntime Application Self-ProtectionAgent inside a deployed app that blocks attacks in real time (preventive).
RBACRole-Based Access ControlPermissions tied to roles; users inherit from their role.
RDPRemote Desktop ProtocolProtocol for remote graphical access to systems.
RESTRepresentational State TransferLightweight, fast API style for web services (often JSON).
RFIDRadio Frequency IdentificationRadio-powered key-card identification for physical access.
RMFRisk Management FrameworkFederal risk approach used with NIST 800-53 / FISMA.
RPORecovery Point ObjectiveMaximum acceptable data loss, measured in time.
RSARivest-Shamir-AdlemanPublic-key crypto; a key type TPMs and HSMs generate and store.
RTORecovery Time ObjectiveMaximum acceptable downtime after a disruption.
S
SaaSSoftware as a ServiceCloud model: provider manages everything; customer just uses the software.
SAMLSecurity Assertion Markup LanguageXML-based attestation format for enterprise SSO/federation.
SAQSelf-Assessment QuestionnairePCI DSS self-assessment for merchant Levels 2–4.
SASESecure Access Service EdgeCombines networking and security (firewall, SWG, ZTNA) into one cloud service.
SASTStatic Application Security TestingAnalyzes source code without running it (Checkmarx, Fortify).
SBOMSoftware Bill of MaterialsInventory of all components and dependencies in software.
SCASoftware Composition AnalysisIdentifies vulnerable third-party dependencies (OWASP Dependency-Check).
SCAPSecurity Content Automation ProtocolAutomates compliance checks against CIS/DoD benchmarks.
SCCMSystem Center Configuration ManagerMicrosoft tool for inventory, software versions, and patching.
SDNSoftware-Defined NetworkingSoftware controls network traffic via control/data/management planes.
SD-WANSoftware-Defined Wide Area NetworkExtends SDN across wide areas; routes branch traffic intelligently.
SEDSelf-Encrypting DriveStorage with an onboard engine; keys never leave the drive hardware.
SELinuxSecurity-Enhanced LinuxMAC implementation built into the Linux kernel (NSA/Red Hat).
SEVSecure Encrypted VirtualizationAMD CPU extension encrypting VM memory, even from a compromised hypervisor.
SFTPSSH File Transfer ProtocolEncrypted file transfer replacing plaintext FTP.
SGXSoftware Guard ExtensionsIntel feature creating isolated secure memory enclaves.
SHASecure Hash AlgorithmHash family; SHA-256 is the current standard, SHA-1 deprecated.
SIEMSecurity Information and Event ManagementAggregates and normalizes logs for correlation and alerting.
SIGStandardized Information GatheringQuestionnaire template for standardizing vendor assessments.
SLAService Level AgreementContract defining uptime, response times, and performance metrics.
SLESingle Loss ExpectancyLoss from one occurrence of a risk: AV × EF.
SOAPSimple Object Access ProtocolStrict XML API style for high-security, reliable transactions.
SOC 2Service Organization Control 2AICPA audit report on a service provider's controls (Type I vs II).
SOXSarbanes-Oxley ActUS law mandating financial reporting accuracy and internal controls.
SPService ProviderApp that delegates authentication to an IdP (Slack, Dropbox, Salesforce).
SPISensitive Personal InformationMore-sensitive subset of PII with stricter handling rules.
SSHSecure ShellEncrypted protocol for remote access and command execution.
SSOSingle Sign-OnAuthenticate once, access many services without re-entering credentials.
STARSecurity, Trust, Assurance, and RiskCSA's two-level cloud provider certification program.
STIGSecurity Technical Implementation GuideDoD hardened configuration standard.
SWGSecure Web GatewayFilters and inspects web traffic; a component bundled into SASE.

TAPTest Access PointDevice that copies network traffic for monitoring (passive or active).
TDETransparent Data EncryptionDatabase-level encryption transparent to applications.
TEETrusted Execution EnvironmentIsolated CPU area shielding sensitive processes from the OS.
TGSTicket Granting ServiceKerberos service that issues per-resource service tickets.
TGTTicket Granting TicketKerberos token proving identity, used to request service tickets.
TLSTransport Layer SecurityEncrypts data in transit; the basis of HTTPS.
TPMTrusted Platform ModuleHardware chip storing keys and supporting disk encryption / secure boot.
TXTTrusted Execution TechnologyIntel CPU extension validating a trusted environment before sensitive apps run.
U
UEFIUnified Extensible Firmware InterfaceModern firmware standard enabling Secure Boot.
V
VDIVirtual Desktop InfrastructureHosted virtual desktops; a deployment target for endpoint agents.
VLANVirtual Local Area NetworkLogical network segment used to isolate traffic, including P2P.
VNetVirtual NetworkAzure private segmented cloud network (the AWS equivalent is VPC).
VPCVirtual Private CloudAWS private segmented cloud network (the Azure equivalent is VNet).
VPNVirtual Private NetworkEncrypted tunnel for secure communication over untrusted networks.
vTPMVirtual Trusted Platform ModuleSoftware-emulated TPM giving each virtual machine its own root of trust.
W

WIDSWireless Intrusion Detection SystemIDS focused on wireless network threats.
WORMWrite Once, Read ManyStorage that cannot be altered after writing; tamper-proof audit trails.
WSUSWindows Server Update ServicesMicrosoft patch management: deploys and verifies updates.
X

XSSCross-Site ScriptingWeb flaw injecting malicious script through unsanitized input.
Z
ZTNAZero Trust Network AccessPer-request, identity-aware access to apps; a core SASE component.

term glossary

The high-value and most-confused concepts across the sections. For side-by-side comparisons (access models, testing types, frameworks), see the cheat sheets.

term meaning
Air-gapped networkNetwork physically isolated from all external connections; highest isolation.
Always-on VPNVPN that auto-connects on startup and stays active regardless of location.
Asset attestationVerifying an asset's security posture/compliance; failing assets are quarantined.
Attack surfaceTotal set of entry points an attacker could exploit; reduced through hardening.
AttestationSigned message from an IdP to an SP confirming a user is authenticated (SAML/JWT).
Bastion hostHardened, minimal-service host that controls and absorbs external access.
BitLockerWindows full-disk encryption (AES), often using a TPM to manage keys.
Conditional accessAccess policy that evaluates context (location, device, time), not just identity.
Continuous authorizationRe-evaluating access permissions throughout a session, not just at login.
Data controllerParty deciding why/how personal data is processed; bears primary GDPR responsibility.
Data custodianIT/security role implementing technical protection of data day to day.
Data ownerBusiness leader accountable for classification and access decisions.
Data processorParty processing personal data on the controller's documented instructions.
Data stewardSubject-matter expert responsible for data quality, meaning, and accuracy.
Data subjectIndividual whose personal data is processed; holds GDPR rights.
Data topology mappingClassifying data into zones (by users, flow, importance) before segmenting.
DeclassificationDowngrading data to a lower classification when it is no longer sensitive.
Defense-in-depthLayering independent controls so one failure does not compromise everything.
Default-denyBlock everything unless explicitly permitted (allowlist); safer posture.
DeperimeterizationSecurity follows the user/device, not the network edge; perimeter is no longer a place.
DeprovisioningRemoving access when no longer needed (role change, departure).
Due careOngoing reasonable steps to prevent harm after a relationship begins.
Due diligenceThorough evaluation of a vendor before entering a partnership.
FederationTrust shared across organizations so home credentials work elsewhere (eduGAIN).
FileVaultmacOS full-disk encryption (AES) built into the operating system.
HardeningReducing attack surface by removing unnecessary services, ports, and accounts.
HashingOne-way function producing a fixed-size fingerprint of input data.
HypervisorSoftware layer between physical hardware and VMs that creates virtual hardware.
Identity proofingVerifying a user's real identity before issuing credentials.
Jump boxHardened gateway admins connect through to reach systems in a secure zone.
Measured BootRecords boot-component hashes to the TPM for attestation; detects but does not block.
Micro-segmentationIsolating individual workloads so a breach cannot move laterally.
Need-to-knowAccess granted only with both clearance and a demonstrated need.
NormalizationSIEM converting diverse log formats into one structure for correlation.
PiggybackingEntering a secure area by being let in by an authorized person (with awareness).
ProvisioningAssigning access rights based on a user's role.
Residual riskRisk remaining after controls are applied.
Risk appetiteOverall amount of risk an organization is willing to take.
Risk toleranceAcceptable variation around the risk appetite for a specific activity.
Root of trustInherently-trusted foundation (TPM/vTPM/HSM) anchoring secure operations.
Screened subnetAnother name for the DMZ: a buffer zone between internal and external networks.
Secure BootFirmware (UEFI) check preventing unauthorized code from loading at startup.
Secure EnclaveApple coprocessor with its own processor/memory for keys and biometric data.
Self-healing hardwareHardware that auto-detects, corrects, and recovers from faults (e.g. RAID rebuild).
Self-provisioningUser-initiated access requests via a portal, then approved by policy or admin.
Separation of dutiesNo single person controls all of a critical process; fraud prevention.
Shadow ITUnauthorized cloud apps or services used outside corporate control.
Shared responsibilitySplit of security duties between cloud provider and customer (varies by model).
Subject-object relationshipRules for how subjects (users/devices/apps) may interact with objects (data/systems).
TailgatingUnauthorized person slips through a secure door behind someone (no awareness).
Tamper-evident logAppend-only record that reveals attempts to alter it.
Transitive trustIf A trusts B and B trusts C, then A trusts C, with no direct A–C agreement.
Trust boundaryPerimeter where organizational control ends and a third party's begins.
Virtual hardwareHypervisor-simulated CPU/memory/storage so many VMs share one server.
Zero trustNever trust by location; verify every user and device continuously.
Zero-dayUnknown vulnerability with no signature yet; scanners relying on CVEs miss it.
