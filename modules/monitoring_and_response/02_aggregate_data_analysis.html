Aggregate Data Analysis

Turning billions of logs into a few thousand that matter: cut the noise, link related events, rank by impact, and watch for patterns over time.

overview

Aggregate data analysis combines and examines large volumes of data from multiple sources to find patterns, trends, and threats. Four concepts: audit log reduction (filter out low-priority logs), correlation (link related events across sources), prioritization (rank by impact), and trends (spot recurring patterns over time).

concept 01

Audit Log Reduction

Filter routine logs to focus on critical events. A tuned SIEM can cut billions of logs to thousands. Watch for false positives.

concept 02

Correlation

Link related events across sources to see the full picture (failed logins + traffic spike = brute force). IBM QRadar, ArcSight.

concept 03

Prioritization

Rank issues by potential impact so the biggest threats get attention first (sensitive-data system over a noncritical one).

concept 04

Trends

Identify recurring patterns over time to find weak spots and act proactively. Splunk, Elasticsearch.

the four concepts

Audit log reduction filters out routine, low-priority logs (successful logins, regular file access) so teams focus on anomalies like repeated login failures. A properly tuned SIEM (e.g. Splunk) can take billions of logs per quarter down to thousands of high-priority ones; the challenge is minimizing false positives.

Correlation links related events across independent sources to reveal context, e.g. repeated failed logins plus an unusual traffic spike from an unfamiliar IP together indicating a brute-force attack (tools: IBM QRadar, ArcSight). Prioritization ranks events by impact: a failed login on a noncritical system can wait, but repeated failures on a system with sensitive customer data take precedence. Trends identify recurring patterns over time (e.g. unauthorized access attempts at certain hours), letting teams strengthen defenses proactively (tools: Splunk, Elasticsearch).

EXAM TIPS

what to know for the exam

  • Four concepts: audit log reduction, correlation, prioritization, trends.
  • Log reduction filters routine logs (billions → thousands via a tuned SIEM); watch for false positives.
  • Correlation links events across sources to detect coordinated attacks (failed logins + traffic spike = brute force).
  • Prioritization ranks by impact; trends spot recurring patterns for proactive defense.
  • SIEMs (Splunk, QRadar, ArcSight) provide reduction, correlation, and prioritization together.
decision rules
  • Scenario filters out routine logs to focus on critical events → audit log reduction
  • Scenario links failed logins to a traffic spike → correlation
  • Scenario addresses the highest-impact threat first → prioritization
  • Scenario notices recurring attacks at a certain time → trends
