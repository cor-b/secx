Governance Frameworks

Structured guidelines for aligning IT operations with business objectives while managing risk and compliance — the "what and why" vs. the "how."

overview

Two frameworks dominate this space: COBIT (governance and management — the strategic layer) and ITIL (IT service management — the operational layer). They are complementary, not competing.

the two frameworks

COBIT Governance & Management Aligns IT with business objectives. 5 domains, 40 objectives. Manager's perspective — the "what and why."
ITIL IT Service Management Optimizes how IT services are delivered. 34 practices. Operational perspective — the "how."

01COBIT

Control Objectives for Information and Related Technologies. A governance and management framework for enterprise IT — broader than security alone. Ensures IT supports business objectives while balancing benefit realization, risk optimization, and resource optimization.

5 domains

Split between one governance domain and four management domains:

governance (1 domain)

EDM — Evaluate, Direct, Monitor
The governance layer Sets strategic direction, oversees performance, and ensures compliance. The "board and executive" domain.

management (4 domains)

APO — Align, Plan, Organize
Strategic planning and organization Ensures IT strategy aligns with enterprise goals and resources are organized appropriately.
BAI — Build, Acquire, Implement
Delivery of new capabilities Covers building, acquiring, and implementing solutions and changes.
DSS — Deliver, Service, Support
Day-to-day operations Execution and support of IT services — keeping things running.
MEA — Monitor, Evaluate, Assess
Ongoing performance monitoring Tracks whether controls are working, evaluates performance, and assesses compliance.

Key numbers

  • 5 domains — 1 governance (EDM) + 4 management (APO, BAI, DSS, MEA)
  • 40 governance and management objectives — each provides practices, activities, metrics, and capability levels

How COBIT is applied

  1. Identify enterprise goals and stakeholder needs Start with what the business requires from IT.
  2. Map to COBIT objectives Translate business goals to alignment goals and relevant COBIT objectives.
  3. Assess current capabilities Identify gaps in governance, risk management, and performance.
  4. Apply COBIT guidance Implement tailored to risk appetite, regulatory requirements, and business priorities.
  5. Monitor using MEA domain Ongoing evaluation and continuous improvement.

02ITIL

Information Technology Infrastructure Library. The world's most widely adopted IT service management framework. Provides best practices for delivering and managing IT services — used primarily by IT operations teams.

Key facts

  • 34 practices total — information security management is one of them (integrated, not the focus)
  • Used primarily by IT operations teams
  • Standard in Fortune 500 and large enterprises

Where ITIL excels

  • Improving service quality and reliability
  • Aligning IT services with business needs
  • Works well with Agile, DevOps, and DevSecOps environments
  • Promotes collaboration between development, operations, and security teams

exam tips

★Key Takeaways

  • Memory hook: COBIT = Controls/Governance (strategic), ITIL = IT service management (operational).
  • EDM is the only governance domain in COBIT — APO, BAI, DSS, MEA are all management domains.
  • COBIT has 5 domains and 40 objectives — memorize both numbers.
  • ITIL has 34 practices — security is one of them, not the focus.
  • ITIL is not a security framework — it includes security as one practice within broader service delivery.
quick decision rules
  • Aligning IT with business goals, risk, or compliance → COBIT
  • IT service delivery, DevOps, operations teams → ITIL
  • "Evaluate, Direct, Monitor" → EDM — the governance domain in COBIT
  • Ongoing monitoring and performance evaluation → MEA domain (COBIT)
