Access, Authentication, Authorization

Managing who can access what, verifying they are who they claim to be, and ensuring they only do what they are permitted to do.

overview

Domain 2, Objective 2.4: given a scenario, apply security to the design of access, authentication, and authorization systems. Topics span the full access lifecycle: the systems that enforce access, how rights are granted and revoked, the models that define who gets access, how identity is verified, the policies that govern conditional access, and the monitoring that ensures compliance.

theme 01

Access Systems

Physical (RFID, biometrics, mantrap) and logical (passwords, MFA, smart cards) mechanisms that enforce who can enter or connect.

theme 02

Access Models

Rule-based (MAC, DAC, ABAC) and role-based (RBAC) models that define the logic behind granting or denying access to resources.

theme 03

Identity & Federation

Identity providers, SSO (Kerberos, Shibboleth), service providers, federation, transitive trust, and attestation via SAML and JWT.

theme 04

Policies & Oversight

Conditional access, policy decision points, policy enforcement points, centralized logging (Syslog, SIEM), and tamper-proof auditing.
