Commercial and Government Challenges

Where the targets are money, secrets, and intellectual property. Three sectors and their landmark attacks: manufacturing, finance, and government/defense.

overview

Commercial and government challenges involve securing sensitive operations and data against sophisticated threats. Three sectors: manufacturing (ICS and intellectual property), finance (transaction systems, customer data, compliance), and government/defense (classified info, critical infrastructure, military operations, often facing state-sponsored attacks).

sector 01

Manufacturing

Protect ICS and intellectual property. 2019 Norsk Hydro (LockerGoga, $40M+); 2014 US Steel IP espionage; growing IoT attack surface.

sector 02

Finance

Secure transactions + customer data; comply (GDPR, PCI DSS). 2016 Bangladesh Bank SWIFT heist ($81M stolen).

sector 03

Government/Defense

Classified info, military comms vs state-sponsored attacks. 2020 SolarWinds supply chain; 2008 Agent.btz USB malware.

manufacturing

Manufacturing must protect ICS (controlling machines and robotics central to production) and intellectual property (designs, trade secrets, processes). The 2019 Norsk Hydro attack (LockerGoga ransomware) forced manual operations, with employees using handwritten notes, costing over $40 million. IP theft is the other concern: in 2014, US Steel was targeted by alleged Chinese state-sponsored espionage aiming to steal production secrets. The rise of IoT in factories also expands the attack surface, mitigated with updated firmware and traffic monitoring.

finance

Finance must secure transaction systems and customer data and ensure regulatory compliance. The 2016 Bangladesh central bank attack is the example: hackers (believed to be North Korea's Lazarus group) exploited weaknesses in the SWIFT payment system, attempting nearly $1 billion and succeeding in transferring $81 million before a typo raised suspicion. Customer data (personal info, card numbers) is a prime target, so institutions must comply with GDPR and PCI DSS; failure brings fines and reputational damage.

government and defense

Government/defense must secure classified information and military operations against state-sponsored attacks. The 2020 SolarWinds attack is the example: hackers inserted malicious code into updates for the SolarWinds Orion network-management platform, gaining undetected access to multiple federal agencies (Homeland Security, Energy, Treasury), a supply-chain compromise. Military communications are also critical: in 2008, the Agent.btz malware infiltrated US military networks via infected USB drives, creating a backdoor; the cleanup led to a ban on USB drives across military networks and helped catalyze the creation of US Cyber Command in 2010.

EXAM TIPS

what to know for the exam

  • Three sectors: manufacturing (ICS + IP), finance (transactions + data + compliance), government/defense (classified info, state-sponsored).
  • Manufacturing: 2019 Norsk Hydro (LockerGoga, manual ops, $40M+); 2014 US Steel IP espionage.
  • Finance: 2016 Bangladesh Bank SWIFT heist ($81M); comply with GDPR and PCI DSS.
  • Government/defense: 2020 SolarWinds (supply-chain via Orion updates); 2008 Agent.btz (USB malware → USB ban, US Cyber Command).
  • SolarWinds is the canonical software supply-chain attack example.
decision rules
  • Scenario steals product designs/trade secrets → manufacturing IP theft
  • Scenario abuses SWIFT to move funds fraudulently → finance (Bangladesh Bank)
  • Scenario compromises trusted software updates → supply-chain (SolarWinds)
  • Scenario spreads via infected USB into military networks → Agent.btz
