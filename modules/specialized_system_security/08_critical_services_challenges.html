Critical Services Challenges

When the systems under attack keep society running. Three sectors where a breach means outages, accidents, or lives at risk: utilities, transportation, and healthcare.

overview

Critical services challenges involve protecting infrastructure whose disruption has major societal impact. Three sectors: utilities (power grids, water, gas), transportation (rail, air traffic control, transit, shipping), and healthcare (medical devices, patient data, hospital networks). Each is illustrated by a real attack.

sector 01

Utilities

Power grids, water, gas via ICS. Compromise → outages or contamination. 2015 Ukraine grid attack cut power to 230,000.

sector 02

Transportation

Rail, air traffic control, transit, shipping. 2017 NotPetya hit Maersk: ~$300M, 10 days, 76 ports.

sector 03

Healthcare

Medical devices, patient data, hospital networks. 2017 WannaCry hit the UK NHS: 19,000 appointments cancelled.

utilities

Utilities protect power grids, water systems, and gas pipelines, where disruption causes widespread outages or water contamination. They rely on industrial control systems; if compromised, attackers can manipulate the flow of electricity, water, or gas. The 2015 Ukrainian power grid attack is the key example: hackers remotely hijacked operator workstations to shut down substations, cutting power to ~230,000 people, while also launching a DoS on the utilities' call centers to block outage reporting. Defenses: firewalls, encryption, regular software updates for ICS, plus security audits and employee training.

transportation

Transportation, rail, air traffic control, public transit, shipping, increasingly relies on digital systems; disruption causes delays, accidents, and risk to life. The 2017 NotPetya ransomware attack on Maersk is the example: it shut down systems for nearly 10 days, impacted 76 ports, and cost ~$300 million. NotPetya spread rapidly by exploiting an unpatched Windows vulnerability, highlighting timely patching and network segmentation. Because Maersk handled ~15-18% of global container shipping, the disruption hit worldwide supply chains. Defenses: network segmentation, IDS, and strong access controls.

healthcare

Healthcare faces unique challenges from sensitive patient data and critical medical devices/networks; attacks can steal health information or disrupt life-saving care. The 2017 WannaCry ransomware attack is the example: it exploited unpatched Windows systems, infecting over 230,000 computers in 150 countries, and hit the UK's NHS, affecting at least 81 trusts, cancelling ~19,000 appointments/procedures, and forcing surgeries to be cancelled and patients diverted. Defenses: securing medical devices, encrypting patient data, MFA for staff, and regular updates/patching.

EXAM TIPS

what to know for the exam

  • Three critical sectors: utilities, transportation, healthcare, disruption has societal impact.
  • Utilities: 2015 Ukraine power grid attack (substations shut down, 230,000 lost power, plus DoS on call centers).
  • Transportation: 2017 NotPetya hit Maersk (~$300M, 76 ports), spread via unpatched Windows.
  • Healthcare: 2017 WannaCry hit the NHS (19,000 appointments cancelled), also unpatched Windows.
  • Common thread: unpatched systems and weak segmentation enabled the worst impacts.
decision rules
  • Scenario manipulates electricity/water flow via ICS → utilities challenge
  • Scenario halts global shipping via ransomware → transportation (NotPetya/Maersk)
  • Scenario cancels surgeries after a ransomware outbreak → healthcare (WannaCry/NHS)
  • Scenario spreads through an unpatched Windows flaw → patch management + segmentation
