Risk Management

Identifying, assessing, and managing enterprise security risk to protect organizational assets and ensure operational resilience.

overview

Risk management protects organizational assets across confidentiality, integrity, availability, and privacy. Comprehensive frameworks and assessment strategies identify and mitigate threats; strategic responses and impact analyses frame how an organization addresses those risks.

Domain 1 (Governance, Risk, Compliance), Objective 1.2: Given a set of organizational security requirements, perform risk management activities.

topics in this section

  1. Confidentiality Risk Considerations Mitigating threats that lead to unauthorized access or disclosure of sensitive information. Incident response testing, encryption, data breach response, and reporting.
  2. Integrity Risk Considerations Protecting data and systems from unauthorized modifications that could compromise accuracy and trustworthiness. Interference, hashing, remote journaling, anti-tampering.
  3. Availability Risk Considerations Ensuring critical systems and data remain accessible during and after disruptive events. Business continuity and disaster recovery planning, backups, BCDR testing.
  4. Privacy Risk Considerations Managing risks specific to personal data and individual rights. Biometrics, data subject rights, data sovereignty.
  5. Risk Assessment Frameworks Structured approaches to identify, assess, and manage risk: NIST RMF, ISO 27005, COSO ERM, OCTAVE, FAIR.
  6. Risk Assessment Identifying, analyzing, and evaluating risk to guide mitigation. Quantitative vs qualitative analysis, risk appetite, risk tolerance, risk prioritization.
  7. Risk Response Implementing controls to address identified risk. Validation, severity impact assessment, remediation strategies.
  8. Impact Analysis Evaluating the potential consequences of risks on operations, assets, and objectives. Extreme but plausible scenarios that drive prioritization.
  9. Third-Party Risk Management Assessing and mitigating risks from external parties. Subprocessor risk, vendor risk, supply chain risk.
