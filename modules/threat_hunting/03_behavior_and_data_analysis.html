Behavior and Data Analysis

Three lenses for finding a breach already underway: spotting the attacker's information-gathering, hunting on educated guesses, and flagging users who deviate from their own norms.

overview

Behavior and data analysis examines patterns and anomalies in system and user activity to detect threats. Three concepts: internal reconnaissance (spotting an attacker gathering info inside the network), hypothesis-based searches (hunting guided by assumptions about attacker behavior), and user behavior analytics (UBA) (flagging deviations from a user's normal activity).

concept 01

Internal Reconnaissance

Attacker gathering info after initial access (port scanning, mapping). A key sign a breach already occurred.

concept 02

Hypothesis-based Searches

Hunting guided by an assumption about how an attacker might operate. Focused, efficient; catches stealthy APTs.

concept 03

User Behavior Analytics

Baseline normal user activity, flag deviations (odd hours, unusual locations). Catches insider threats, compromised accounts.

internal reconnaissance

Internal reconnaissance is what an attacker does after gaining initial access but before continuing, gathering info on the internal network (vulnerabilities, privileges, critical assets) by scanning ports, identifying services, and locating sensitive data. Because threat-hunting assumes a breach has occurred, spotting internal reconnaissance is a key indicator a breach already happened. Hunters analyze network logs for unusual scanning, anomalous access to sensitive resources, or a sudden spike in port scanning from an unknown device, then act before the attacker escalates.

hypothesis-based searches

Hypothesis-based searches begin with an assumption or theory about how an attacker might be operating (e.g. "attackers are exploiting a known web-app vulnerability"), then look for evidence to support or disprove it. This is focused and strategic rather than blindly searching all data, making investigations efficient. It's especially good at detecting advanced persistent threats (APTs) that evade traditional alerts. Even if the hypothesis is wrong, the hunter learns whether their defenses would have caught that attack.

user behavior analytics (UBA)

UBA analyzes user activity to detect deviations from a baseline of normal behavior, tracking interactions over time and alerting on anomalies like accessing sensitive data at odd hours or logging in from unusual locations. It's powerful for insider threats and compromised accounts, catching subtle indicators that signature-based systems miss because the malicious activity is performed by legitimately issued credentials. Example: an account suddenly downloading large volumes from a sensitive database late at night gets flagged for investigation.

EXAM TIPS

what to know for the exam

  • Threat-hunting assumes a breach; internal reconnaissance is a key sign it already happened.
  • Internal reconnaissance = attacker mapping the network after access (port scans, unusual resource access).
  • Hypothesis-based searches are focused/strategic, guided by an assumption; good for stealthy APTs.
  • UBA baselines normal user behavior and flags deviations (odd hours, locations), catching insider threats and compromised accounts.
  • UBA catches what signature-based detection misses because legit credentials are used.
decision rules
  • Scenario sees port scanning from an internal device post-breach → internal reconnaissance
  • Scenario hunts based on a theory about a specific exploit → hypothesis-based search
  • Scenario flags a user downloading data at 3am → user behavior analytics
  • Scenario detects misuse by a legitimate account → UBA (insider/compromised account)
