Threat-hunting

Assume breach, then go looking. Proactively searching the network for signs of malicious activity using behavior analysis, internal and external intelligence, shared indicators, and deception.

overview

Domains 2 and 4, Objectives 2.3 (integrate controls into a secure architecture) and 4.3 (apply threat-hunting and threat intelligence concepts). Threat-hunting is proactive: it assumes breach and searches for signs of malicious activity that traditional defenses miss. The section covers indicators of attack, behavior/data analysis, internal and external intelligence sources, detection enablers, threat intelligence platforms, IoC sharing, rule-based languages, and counterintelligence/operational security.

area 01

Hunt

Indicators of attack (TTPs), behavior and data analysis, internal intelligence sources, detection and threat-hunting enablers.

area 02

Intelligence

External intelligence sources, threat intelligence platforms (TIPs), IoC sharing (STIX/TAXII/AIS).

area 03

Detect & Deceive

Rule-based languages (Sigma/YARA/Rita/Snort), counterintelligence and operational security (cyber deception).
