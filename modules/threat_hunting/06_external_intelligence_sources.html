External Intelligence Sources

Intelligence from outside your walls: what's publicly findable (OSINT), industry sharing groups (ISACs), grading source trust (the Admiralty Scale), and watching the dark web.

overview

External intelligence sources are data and insights gathered from outside the organization. Four concepts: OSINT (publicly available information), ISACs (industry information-sharing centers), reliability factors (assessing source trustworthiness, the Admiralty Scale), and dark web monitoring (tracking criminal activity and leaks).

source 01

OSINT

Publicly available info (websites, social media, forums). See your exposure as attackers do; study their TTPs. Maltego, Shodan.

source 02

ISACs

Industry-specific info-sharing centers (FS-ISAC, Health-ISAC). Pool threat intelligence across a sector.

source 03

Reliability Factors

Assess source trust. Admiralty Scale: source A-F, info 1-6 (A1 best, F6 worst). Cross-verify.

source 04

Dark Web Monitoring

Track criminal marketplaces for leaked data/targeting (DarkOwl). Early breach warning.

OSINT and ISACs

OSINT is the collection of publicly available information (websites, social media, forums). It lets an organization see its external exposure as attackers do (tools: Maltego, Shodan) and also study attackers' TTPs by monitoring threat-actor forums and exploit databases. ISACs (Information Sharing and Analysis Centers) facilitate cybersecurity collaboration within specific industries, pooling resources, e.g. FS-ISAC (financial), Health-ISAC, Energy-ISAC, Aviation-ISAC. They provide threat reports, alerts, and best practices; some have paid tiers for deeper, real-time intelligence. Sharing means a threat seen by one member helps all members respond faster.

reliability factors and dark web monitoring

Reliability factors assess the credibility and trustworthiness of intelligence sources, ensuring information is accurate and timely. The Admiralty Scale assigns two ratings: source reliability A-F and information accuracy 1-6 (where A1 is a highly reliable source with confirmed info, F6 is unreliable with unverified info). This prevents acting on bad intelligence (which wastes resources or causes disruption); organizations also use multiple sources and cross-verification. Dark web monitoring tracks hidden parts of the internet where criminals sell stolen data, hacking services, and vulnerabilities (tool: DarkOwl); finding your credentials for sale signals a breach, providing an early warning to reset passwords and investigate.

EXAM TIPS

what to know for the exam

  • Four external sources: OSINT, ISACs, reliability factors, dark web monitoring.
  • OSINT = publicly available info (Maltego, Shodan); shows your exposure and attackers' TTPs.
  • ISACs = industry-specific sharing groups (FS-ISAC, Health-ISAC).
  • The Admiralty Scale rates source reliability (A-F) and information accuracy (1-6); A1 best, F6 worst.
  • Dark web monitoring (DarkOwl) gives early warning of leaked data or targeting.
decision rules
  • Scenario gathers publicly available info with Shodan → OSINT
  • Scenario shares threat data within a financial-sector group → ISAC (FS-ISAC)
  • Scenario grades a feed A1 vs F6 → reliability factors (Admiralty Scale)
  • Scenario finds company credentials for sale online → dark web monitoring
