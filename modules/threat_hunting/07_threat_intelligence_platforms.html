Threat Intelligence Platforms (TIPs)

Tools that gather third-party threat feeds and cross-reference them against your own logs, so external knowledge sharpens internal detection and prioritizes what matters to you.

overview

Threat intelligence platforms (TIPs) gather, analyze, and distribute threat data to help detect and respond to risks. They pull intelligence from third-party vendors (Recorded Future, FireEye, CrowdStrike) and cross-reference external intelligence with internal logs to improve detection. They integrate with existing security infrastructure, prioritize relevant threats, and work best when automated.

capability 01

Aggregate Third-party Feeds

Pull intelligence from vendors (Recorded Future, FireEye, CrowdStrike). Open-source (flexible/community) or proprietary (curated/premium).

capability 02

Cross-reference Internal Data

Match external intel against internal logs/alerts for precise detection and faster response (e.g. phishing in email logs).

capability 03

Integrate & Prioritize

Auto-update firewalls/EDR/email gateways with IOCs; prioritize threats relevant to your industry.

how TIPs work

A TIP's power is integrating third-party intelligence into the enterprise and cross-referencing it with internal data. Example: a TIP pulls intelligence from FireEye about a new malware strain; if internal email logs show unusual activity, the TIP cross-references them to confirm targeting and provide actionable insights. Feeds can be open-source (flexible, community-driven) or proprietary (premium, curated, advanced integration). A practical case: FireEye identifies a phishing campaign using compromised accounts, the TIP cross-checks internal email traffic against known malicious addresses/attachment hashes and blocks matching emails before they reach users. TIPs also help detect long-term attacks (e.g. APT tactics from Recorded Future) by monitoring for lateral movement or exfiltration patterns.

automation and prioritization

TIPs should be automated: configure them to automatically update firewalls, endpoint detection, and email security gateways with the latest IOCs from third-party vendors, keeping defenses current. A key benefit is prioritization: not all threats matter equally to every organization, so a TIP helps a healthcare org prioritize a healthcare-system exploit over less relevant threats, allocating resources to the most critical risks first. A proprietary feed (e.g. CrowdStrike) can feed directly into a SIEM for more accurate, contextual alerts.

EXAM TIPS

what to know for the exam

  • TIPs aggregate third-party threat feeds (Recorded Future, FireEye, CrowdStrike) and cross-reference with internal logs.
  • Feeds are open-source (flexible, community) or proprietary (premium, curated).
  • Cross-referencing external intel with internal data gives precise detection and faster response.
  • Automate TIPs to update firewalls/EDR/email gateways with the latest IOCs.
  • TIPs help prioritize threats most relevant to your industry.
decision rules
  • Scenario aggregates vendor feeds and matches them to internal logs → threat intelligence platform
  • Scenario auto-updates the email gateway with IOCs to block phishing → TIP automation
  • Scenario focuses on a healthcare exploit over others → TIP prioritization
  • Scenario feeds CrowdStrike intel into a SIEM for context → TIP integration
