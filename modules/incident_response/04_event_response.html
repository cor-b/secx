Event Response

After containment: piecing together what happened, restoring what was lost, and meeting the legal obligations that follow a breach.

overview

Event response is the work done to handle, investigate, and mitigate an incident after it has been detected and contained. Three concepts: timeline reconstruction (rebuilding the sequence of events from logs), data recovery and extraction (restoring lost data and pulling evidence), and breach response (mitigating damage, securing systems, and meeting legal/regulatory duties like notification).

concept 01

Timeline Reconstruction

Builds a chronological account from logs to find the attack path and exploited vulnerabilities. SIEM + forensic tools; watch for log tampering.

concept 02

Data Recovery & Extraction

Restores lost/compromised data and extracts evidence (compromised credentials, exfiltrated files) for forensic analysis.

concept 03

Breach Response

Mitigates damage, secures systems, and fulfills legal duties: stakeholder/regulator notification under GDPR/CCPA.

timeline reconstruction

Timeline reconstruction gathers data from logs, alerts, and monitoring tools into a chronological account of the attack, revealing the attack path, exploited vulnerabilities, and affected systems (NIST SP 800-61 guides this). Key tools: SIEM systems to centralize logs, and forensic tools like Autopsy or EnCase for device-level detail. Investigators look for anomalies, unusual login times, high-frequency access to sensitive files, atypical admin use.

Attackers may tamper with logs (false timestamps, deleted entries), so reconstruction often correlates logs across independent sources (network, application, endpoint). If a critical action appears in one log but not another that should record it, that suggests tampering. Log gaps (unexplained breaks with no entries) hint at deletions. Tools like Splunk and LogRhythm automate the cross-comparison.

data recovery and extraction

This restores compromised or lost data and extracts useful evidence. Guidance comes from NIST SP 800-61, NIST SP 800-184 (Cybersecurity Event Recovery), and the CISA Incident Response & Recovery Playbook. Tools include Disk Drill and Stellar Data Recovery for damaged/encrypted drives, and Cellebrite for mobile devices. Recovery starts by securing backup copies before restoring; extraction isolates key data (compromised credentials, exfiltrated files) to give forensics clear targets and build an evidence trail.

breach response

Breach response covers mitigating damage, securing compromised systems, and fulfilling legal/regulatory obligations. Frameworks: NIST SP 800-61 and ISO 27035 (Information Security Incident Management). Tools: EDR like CrowdStrike or Carbon Black to isolate infected endpoints, and vulnerability scanners like Nessus to find the weakness that allowed the breach. The team contains and removes the attacker, resets credentials, and ensures secure backups, then notifies stakeholders and regulators, which is often legally required under GDPR or CCPA, to maintain transparency, ensure compliance, and reduce penalties.

EXAM TIPS

what to know for the exam

  • Event response happens AFTER detection and containment: reconstruct, recover, respond to the breach.
  • Timeline reconstruction correlates logs across independent sources to spot tampering; log gaps suggest deletions. SIEM + Autopsy/EnCase.
  • Data recovery secures backups first, then restores; extraction pulls evidence. Cellebrite = mobile device extraction.
  • Breach response includes legally-required notification under GDPR/CCPA. ISO 27035 and NIST SP 800-61 are the frameworks.
  • EDR (CrowdStrike, Carbon Black) isolates infected endpoints during breach response.
decision rules
  • Scenario involves building a chronological account of the attack from logs → timeline reconstruction
  • Scenario involves detecting altered logs by comparing multiple sources → log correlation / tamper detection
  • Scenario involves restoring lost files and pulling evidence from a phone → data recovery & extraction (Cellebrite)
  • Scenario involves notifying regulators within a required timeframe after a breach → breach response (GDPR/CCPA)
