Security Reviews

Audits, assessments, and certifications give organizations structured ways to evaluate and validate security posture against internal policies, regulations, and industry standards.

overview

Security reviews can be internal (conducted by the organization's own team) or external (conducted by independent third parties). Internal reviews focus on identifying weaknesses and improving processes. External reviews provide objective compliance validation, and their results are typically shared with regulators and clients.

The three review types are audits, assessments, and certifications. Audits verify compliance. Assessments explore the overall security posture. Certifications provide formal recognition that an organization has met a specific standard.

three review types

Audits Formal compliance evaluations. Internal audits check adherence to internal policies. External audits verify compliance with regulations and standards like PCI DSS.
Assessments Broader, exploratory reviews that identify vulnerabilities, risks, and gaps in security controls. Can be internal or external. Often include penetration testing.
Certifications Formal validation granted by an accredited certifying body after a rigorous audit or assessment. Provides public recognition that a specific standard has been met.

audits

internal audits

Internal audits are conducted by the organization's own cybersecurity or compliance teams. The primary goal is to verify that internal security policies and procedures are being followed.

  • Focuses on internal compliance: access controls, patch management, policy adherence
  • Identifies risks and weaknesses before they can be exploited
  • Results are not normally required to be shared externally

external audits

External audits are carried out by independent third-party auditors who assess compliance with external regulations, standards, or contractual obligations. They are more formal than internal audits.

  • Provides an objective assessment of compliance with standards like PCI DSS or HIPAA
  • Results are shared with regulators, clients, or stakeholders
  • Required in many regulated industries as proof of compliance

assessments

internal assessments

Internal assessments are broader and more exploratory than internal audits. Rather than checking policy compliance, they evaluate overall security posture and identify potential risks before they can be exploited.

  • Often conducted before launching a new system or migrating to the cloud
  • Surfaces issues like insecure APIs or weak encryption before deployment
  • Results used internally to guide remediation and control improvements

external assessments

External assessments are conducted by cybersecurity consultants or third-party experts who evaluate the organization from an outside perspective. They provide an impartial view of security gaps.

  • Often include penetration testing to identify exploitable vulnerabilities
  • Frequently used when preparing for an external audit or when an impartial review is needed
  • Example: a healthcare company hiring an external firm to run a pen test and verify HIPAA compliance

certifications

Certifications are granted by an accredited certifying body after a rigorous audit or assessment confirms that an organization meets a specific standard. They provide formal, verifiable recognition that can be shared with customers and regulators.

  • All major cloud providers (AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud) hold ISO 27001 certifications
  • Customers in regulated industries can verify certifications to confirm their cloud infrastructure meets required standards
  • AWS Artifact is a self-service portal for accessing AWS compliance documentation, including certifications and audit reports
  • Critical in healthcare and finance where demonstrating HIPAA or PCI DSS adherence is mandatory

exam tips

key takeaways

  • Audits focus on compliance; assessments are broader and exploratory. If a scenario describes identifying gaps in overall security posture, that is an assessment, not an audit.
  • Internal audit results are not normally required to be shared. External audit results are shared with regulators, clients, and stakeholders.
  • External assessments often include penetration testing. Pen tests are a common form of external assessment, especially in regulated industries preparing for an audit.
  • Certifications come from accredited certifying bodies, not from self-declaration. ISO 27001 certifications held by AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud are the canonical exam example.
  • AWS Artifact is the portal for accessing AWS compliance documentation and certifications. Know the name for the exam.
quick decision rules
  • Checking internal policy adherence → Internal audit
  • Regulatory or contractual compliance by a third party → External audit
  • Pre-launch or pre-migration risk review → Internal assessment
  • Pen testing, impartial outside security review → External assessment
  • ISO 27001, formal recognition of meeting a standard → Certification
